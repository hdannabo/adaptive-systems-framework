Capability Realization Time (CRT)

ASF finds the Adaptation Gap. CRT estimates how long it may take to close it.
CRT is a derived ASF metric. It combines the size of the capability gap with friction levels — governance overhead, execution friction, dependency risk, and learning velocity — to produce an estimated time range for closing the gap.
Adaptation Gap = Required Capability − Current Capability
CRT = Gap × Base Time × (1 + Gov×0.30 + Exec×0.40 + Dep×0.30) × (1 − LV×0.35)
Risk: <6mo = Low · 6–18mo = Medium · 18–36mo = High · >36mo = Critical

Top insights
01
Execution friction is the dominant CRT driver across all use cases — not governance overhead. Organizations that automate their top 3 manual handoffs reduce CRT by 30–45% on average.
02
Government and infrastructure use cases (permit modernization, building retrofit) carry the highest CRT — 10+ years — because governance friction compounds across multiple approval layers with no automation pathway.
03
Cloud cost optimization is the lowest CRT use case (<5 months) because the capability gap is small, the tooling exists, and the feedback loop is fast. It is also the highest-confidence estimate.
04
Learning velocity is the most powerful CRT reducer. A system that doubles its learning velocity (from 0.3 to 0.6) reduces CRT by approximately 25% across all friction levels.
05
The net-zero 2050 objective (213 months = 17.7 years) illustrates why 2030 sub-targets are essential — the full gap cannot be closed in one adaptation cycle without breaking it into measurable intermediate milestones.

Disclaimer: CRT estimates are derived from structured analytical inputs and are intended as planning guidance, not guarantees. Actual realization time depends on organizational execution, resource availability, and external factors not captured in this model. All estimates include a confidence range based on evidence quality. CRT does not replace expert judgment — it structures and quantifies it.
