# What Is ASF?

## One sentence

ASF tells you whether your organization is moving fast enough to hit its targets — and if not, exactly what is slowing it down and what to fix first.

---

## The problem ASF solves

Every year, organizations set ambitious targets — AI savings, digital transformation milestones, sustainability commitments, revenue growth.

Most miss them.

Not because the strategy was wrong. Not because the budget was insufficient.

**Because the organization could not move fast enough.**

The gap between recognizing that change is needed and actually delivering that change — that is what most frameworks do not measure. Strategy frameworks tell you where to go. Performance dashboards tell you where you are. Neither tells you whether you are moving fast enough to get there before it matters.

ASF measures that speed. And when the speed is insufficient, it identifies the specific constraint causing the delay and recommends what to do about it.

---

## What ASF tells a CEO in one output

> "Your AI transformation program will likely miss its $4B savings target by $1.2B.
> The bottleneck is not AI capability or budget — it is workforce adoption.
> 58% of your employees have access to the tools and are not using them.
> At current adoption velocity, you will reach $2.8B by 2030.
> To close the gap: launch an adoption program targeting unadopted employees by Q3 2026.
> Every 10% adoption increase adds approximately $250M toward the target."

That is the output ASF is designed to produce. Not a score. A decision.

## How it is different from other frameworks

| Framework | What it measures | What it does not measure |
|---|---|---|
| Balanced Scorecard | Outcomes — financial, customer, process, growth | Whether you're moving fast enough to hit future targets |
| Six Sigma / DMAIC | Quality of existing processes | Speed of changing processes when disruption hits |
| CMMI | Maturity level of processes | Velocity of moving between maturity levels |
| McKinsey 7S | Alignment across organizational elements | Time cost of that misalignment |
| TOGAF | Architecture design quality | How fast the architecture can actually change |
| **ASF** | **Adaptation velocity — how fast you move from current to required state** | **Not a replacement for any of the above** |

ASF does not replace these frameworks. It answers a question none of them address:

> **"Given our current organizational speed, will we hit our target on time?"**

## The core model

Every organization that needs to change moves through the same cycle:

```
Signal arrives → Detect it → Decide what to do → Implement → See results → Learn → Adapt
```

ASF measures the speed at each stage:

| Stage | ASF Dimension | What slows it |
|---|---|---|
| Detect | Observation Latency | No telemetry, delayed reporting, fragmented data |
| Decide | Decision Latency | Too many approval layers, ownership ambiguity, risk aversion |
| Implement | Execution Latency | Legacy systems, manual processes, coordination overhead |
| See results | Feedback Delay | Lagging KPIs, annual reviews, no real-time measurement |
| Learn | Learning Velocity | No postmortems, knowledge silos, repeated mistakes |
| Dependencies | Dependency Index | Everything requires human approval before anything moves |

The dimension with the highest friction score is the primary bottleneck.
Fixing the primary bottleneck has the highest impact on reaching the target on time.

---

## The key finding from 100 cases

Across every organization and every industry studied:

**The bottleneck is almost never awareness.**

Boeing knew their production rate was too low. AT&T knew their AI adoption was insufficient. Kodak knew digital photography was coming.

The failure is always in what happens after awareness — the speed of decision, the friction of execution, the delay before feedback arrives.

This is the Adaptation Gap. ASF measures it. ASF identifies where it is. ASF tells you what to do about it.

---

## What ASF is not

- ASF is not a replacement for strategy
- ASF is not a compliance framework
- ASF is not an audit tool
- ASF does not tell you what your goals should be
- ASF does not provide investment advice

ASF takes your stated goals as given and assesses whether your current organizational velocity is sufficient to achieve them.

---

## Evidence basis

All publicly available company analyses in ASF are derived from:
- Annual reports and SEC filings
- Investor presentations and earnings calls
- Official press releases and sustainability reports
- Published research and analyst coverage

All scores are analytical estimates based on this public information. They are not audited results. Evidence for each score is documented. Confidence levels are stated.
