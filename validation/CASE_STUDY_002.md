**Classification:** Public evidence only

**Date:** June 2026

## The Single Question ASF Answers

> Can LTM realize the commercial and delivery capabilities required to achieve 2X revenue by FY31 (Lakshya 2031) before AI-native competitors establish the enterprise accounts LTM needs?

---

## Section 1 — Strategic Objective

**Publicly stated:** Lakshya 2031 — double revenue from $4.76B (FY26) to approximately $9.5B by FY31. Achieve 200 basis points of EBIT margin expansion (15.4% → 17.4%). Establish BlueVerse as the primary AI delivery platform generating 25%+ of total revenue.

**Source:** LTM Investor Day June 2 2026 (publicly presented). *"Lakshya 2031 commits LTM to doubling its revenue and expanding margins over the next five years, driven by our AI-native BlueVerse ecosystem and outcome-based pricing model."*

**Strategic importance:** 2X revenue requires 15% CAGR sustained over 5 years. FY26 delivered 6% CAGR. The gap between required and current adaptation velocity is 9 percentage points per year. This is not a strategy problem — Lakshya 2031 is a credible target in a growing market. It is a realization speed problem.

**Current state (June 2026):** $4.76B revenue FY26 (+6% YoY). BFSI vertical (33% of revenue) declined 3.9% YoY. BlueVerse outcome-based pricing contracts: 3 of 100 target. Women in workforce: 30.8% of 40% target.

Quarterly earnings disclose detailed vertical-level performance. Management recognized the BFSI decline in Q3FY25, not Q4FY25 or Q1FY26.

**Source evidence:**
- LTM Investor Day June 2 2026: *"We launched BlueVerse in anticipation of the market's shift to outcome-based AI services — we did not wait for clients to demand it."*
- LTM Q3FY25 earnings: Management flagged BFSI headwinds proactively with a rebalancing strategy disclosed in the same quarter the decline was first measurable.
- LTM Q4FY26 earnings: Quarterly vertical breakdown disclosed — BFSI -3.9%, Consumer +20.3%, Manufacturing +12.2% — demonstrating granular real-time tracking.

**Interpretation:** Score 2 reflects fast, proactive signal detection. Score 1 would require CEO-level real-time market dashboards driving weekly pivots. Score 2 is appropriate for a company that consistently identifies market shifts 2–3 quarters before competitors are forced to respond.

## Section 4 — Capability Realization Time (CRT)

**Adaptation Gap:** 4.0 capability points (current 6.0, required 10.0)  
**Domain base time:** 4.0 months per capability point (IT services — software/platform delivery)

```
CRT = Gap × Base × Friction_multiplier × Learning_accelerator × Confidence_adjustment

Friction_multiplier = 1 + (0.35 × 0.30) + (0.55 × 0.40) + (0.40 × 0.30)
                    = 1 + 0.105 + 0.220 + 0.120 = 1.445

Learning_accelerator = 1 − (0.60 × 0.35) = 0.790

Confidence_adjustment = 1 + ((1 − 0.80) × 0.20) = 1.040

CRT = 16.0 × 1.445 × 0.790 × 1.040 = 19.0 months
```

**CRT: 19.0 months (Range: 14.6 – 23.4 months) | Risk: High**  
**Confidence:** High (80%) — NSE-listed with quarterly disclosure  

**What this means:** LTM has approximately 19 months to close its capability gap at current velocity. Lakshya 2031 is 60 months away. The CRT is well within the planning horizon. However, the CRT measures the gap between current and required capability at current velocity — and current revenue velocity (6% CAGR) is insufficient for 2X by FY31. The interpretation: the organizational machine is capable; the commercial execution is the binding constraint.

## Section 5 — Executive Summary

**CEO-readable in under 90 seconds:**

LTM's organizational machinery works. It detects market shifts early, makes fast decisions, and demonstrates measurable learning each quarter. Fit4Future delivered 90 basis points of margin improvement in one year. BlueVerse launched four product extensions in 10 months.

**What is happening:** The problem is not the strategy. The problem is that the Lakshya 2031 commitment requires 15% revenue CAGR and the current run rate is 6%. That 9-point gap compounds annually. BFSI — 33% of revenue — declined 3.9% this year while the broader market for BFSI AI services grew. BlueVerse outcome pricing has 3 contracts of the 100 needed to generate non-linear revenue. At current trajectory, LTM reaches approximately $6.5B by FY31, not $9.5B.

**What to do:**

1. Publish a BFSI vertical recovery plan with quarterly milestones. The absence of a published plan signals either no plan exists or management does not believe it is achievable. Either signals poorly to the market.

2. Disclose BlueVerse outcome-based contract count quarterly. Currently not disclosed separately. External accountability accelerates internal adoption.

3. Apply the same operational discipline to AI delivery adoption (35% → 75% target) as Fit4Future applied to margin improvement. The playbook is proven. Apply it to the right metric.

**Priority:** High. The organizational capability to hit Lakshya 2031 exists. The execution velocity does not yet match the commitment.

## Section 6 — Outcome Validation Opportunity

LTM publishes quarterly earnings with full vertical disclosure. The test is specific and observable:

1. **BFSI recovery test:** Does BFSI return to positive YoY growth within two quarters? ASF identified BFSI execution as the critical constraint. If BFSI returns to growth, the bottleneck identification is partially confirmed.

2. **BlueVerse adoption test:** If LTM begins disclosing BlueVerse outcome-based contract count quarterly, and that count grows to 10+ contracts within 4 quarters, the execution bottleneck is being addressed.

3. **Revenue CAGR test:** If FY27 revenue growth accelerates toward 10%+, the low ALS (2.05) is validated — the organization can accelerate because the machinery is capable.

4. **CRT validation:** At 19-month CRT (High risk), the range means LTM should demonstrate capability gap closure by Q1 2028. If revenue trajectory shifts materially by that date, CRT for IT services in this profile is calibrated.

**Observable confirmation signals:**
- LTM quarterly earnings: BFSI vertical YoY growth rate
