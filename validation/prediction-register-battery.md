## Prediction Issuance Protocol

Predictions are issued when:
1. A specific, observable claim can be made with explicit evidence basis
2. A clear validation date can be set (6–18 months from issuance)
3. A specific, unambiguous observable trigger exists that would confirm or refute the claim
4. The claim is not already confirmed by existing public data

Predictions are not issued for:
- Subjective assessments ("company will perform better/worse")
- Claims without observable triggers
- Short-term market price or quarterly earnings predictions (outside ASF scope)

**Prediction modification policy:** Issued predictions are not modified. If context changes materially after issuance, a new prediction superseding the original may be issued — with explicit reference to the original and the reason for issuance.
